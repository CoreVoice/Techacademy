Site palette — techademycampus.com

Client-mandated · violet · orange · green · slate
Constraint, not choice — these are the colours already live on techademycampus.com, so the brand reads consistent with the existing product.
Violet-led on white — a modern, tech-forward system: one violet primary, orange for warmth, green for success, slate for everything structural.
Expanded, not invented — each base colour gets a light tint and a darker step so it can carry chips, states and hovers without adding new hues.
Applied to Style 2 — mapped onto the Modern layout, since a violet-on-white palette fits it far better than the warm/heritage direction.

How to use it
Violet is the one action colour. Buttons, links, active nav, focus rings and the single most important number per view. One violet moment per screen — don't scatter it, or it stops meaning "act here".
Orange carries warmth & secondary emphasis. Secondary CTAs, badges and highlights. Amber signals in-progress / warning; deep orange signals urgent / high. Keep it supporting, never competing with violet for the primary action.
Green means resolved. Ready, matched, cleared, audit-ready — positive states only. Never decorative, or "success" loses its signal.
Slate does the heavy lifting. Ink text on white, slate for secondary copy, border + muted for structure. Roughly 90% of every screen is neutral so the three brand hues stay loud.
Light tints are for fills, never text. Use the light step behind chips, cards and section bands; keep the solid step for the mark, the tint for the surface.
One gap to flag: the site palette has no dedicated danger red — we're signalling critical/high with deep orange. If hard error / critical states need a stronger cue, we should confirm adding a red before finalising.
